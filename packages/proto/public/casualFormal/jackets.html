Jackets

Suit

Designed to be worn as part of a matching set with pants made from the same fabric. It is typically made of finer fabrics such as wool or silk blends. Suit jackets are more formal in appearance. These jackets are a staple in both business and formal settings.

  • Suit jackets have strong structural construction, allowing for more refined silhouettes.
  • Collars: Typically single-breasted.
  • Suit Jackets are lined in slippery fabrics such as silk or polyester for ease of slip on and off. Suit Jackets may opt to have different hardware and notions.
 Fleece

A synthetic, insulating fabric jacket popular for its lightweight and moisture-wicking properties. These jackets are popular in colder, humid environments.

  • Provides warmth without weight, but prone to pilling and difficult to machine-wash. Linings are recommended to prevent excess heat and lingering odors.
 Denim

Heavyweight cotton canvas jackets or great durability and ease of cleaning.

  • Construction typically follows that of a suit jacket without lining or structuring.
  • Notions: Hardware usually includes metal rivets and buttons to reinforce pockets. Traditionally worn in heavier-duty uses.
 Leather

Heavyweight animal hide jackets of various finishes.

  • Linings: Synthetic or silk linings make for a more breathable leather jacket.
  • Notions: Hardware such as buttons are popular for a more polished look.
  • Leather jackets are wind and water-resistant; however, they should avoid excess water and moisture to increase longevity.
