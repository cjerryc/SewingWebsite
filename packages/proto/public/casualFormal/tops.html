Tops

Button-Ups
A shirt characterized by a full-length button closure from the collar to the hem, allowing for a polished and professional appearance. These shirts are a staple in both business and formal settings.
  • Full Front Panel Button Placket: Buttons run from the top to the bottom, so the shirt may be fully buttoned or partially open.
  • Collars: Square and Pointed are typical of Button-ups.
    • Button-Downs
    A shirt characterized by a full-length button closure from the collar to the hem with extra buttons on the collar, allowing for a polished and professional appearance. These shirts are a staple in both business and formal settings.
  • Full Front Panel Button Placket: Buttons run from the top to the bottom, so the shirt may be fully buttoned or partially open.
  • Collars: Square and Pointed are typical of Button-Downs. There are additional, smaller buttons on the front panel to fasten down the collar onto the main body piece.
    • T-Shirts
    Typically lightweight, breathable, and stretchy, these informal shirts are ideal for casual wear.
  • Sleeves: Sleeves may be pointed or round, true-to-proportion or dropped depending on the style. T-shirt sleeves are usually short sleeves.
  • Collars: T-shirts do not feature collars.
    • Casual Shirts
    Typically lightweight, breathable, and a step-down from more formal shirts like the Button-up or Button-down, these informal shirts are ideal for casual wear.
  • Sleeves: Sleeves may be short sleeves or long sleeves.
  • Colors and Patterns: Casual shirts enjoy more freedom in the variety of colors and patterns available, as compared to its formal counterparts.
  • Collars: Casual Shirts may have collars, or round or v-necklines.
    • Pairs well with bottoms.
    Pairs well with Coats.
    Pairs well with Jackets.
